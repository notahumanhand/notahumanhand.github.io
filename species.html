Dwarf

Dwarves were created by Conzotl, the god of minerals and resources. The original dwarves were much taller, with skin resembling rock formations, but current-day dwarves are shorter than humans with natural skin tones. They range from 4-5 feet tall, and are stouter in stature, with most (if not all) dwarves growing long and thick beards, which have a lot of cultural meaning tied to them. Dwarves are size Small.

Primary Trait: Strong Blood

Whenever a dwarf is healed for any amount of Health, they heal 1 additional Health.

Secondary Trait: Dwarven Fortitude

Dwarves have resistance against Toxic damage.

Tertiary Trait: Bureaucratic

Surface dwarves get a +2 circumstantial bonus to C-Deception, C-Persuasion, and C-Insight checks when dealing with nobility or politics.

Tertiary Trait: Artisans

Mountain dwarves get a +2 circumstantial bonus to checks regarding dwarven artistry, including stonework, metalwork, and brewing.

Tertiary Trait: Subterrestrial

Deep dwarves can see in darkness out to a range of 60 feet.

Gnome

Gnomes were created by Ehrendil, the goddess of nature and wildlife. There are two gnome subspecies: forest and shadow. Forest gnomes are slightly larger, closer to 4 feet tall, while shadow gnomes stand a bit over 3 feet. Gnomes have pointed ears and colourful hair, and tend to have long names due to their naming culture. Gnomes are size Small.

Primary Trait: One with Nature

Forest gnomes gain Druidic as a language option, and get a +3 circumstantial bonus on C-Insight checks on animals.

Primary Trait: One with Shadow

Shadow gnomes gain Undercommon as a language option, and get a +3 circumstantial bonus on K-World checks involving the Underdark.

Secondary Trait: Cunning

Gnomes get a +1 natural bonus to their Magical Defence.

Tertiary Trait: Natural Lore

Forest gnomes get a +1 to the skill level of K-Nature or K-Medicine.

Tertiary Trait: Eyes of the Dark

Shadow gnomes can see in darkness out to a range of 30 feet.

Halfling

Halflings are children of Khisteus, the deity of weather. There are two halfling sub-types: Sun, and storm, reflecting two of the most common temperaments of their deity. Halflings are shorter humanoids, tending to range from 3-4 feet tall. Halflings are size Small.

Primary Trait: Nimble

Halflings get a +1 natural bonus to their base movement. They also get a +1 natural bonus to any one Agility skill.

Secondary Trait: Hard to Hit

Halflings get a +1 natural bonus to their Physical Defence.

Tertiary Trait: Stormy Disposition

Storm halflings get a +1 to the skill level of either C-Intimidation or C-Deception.

Tertiary Trait: Sunny Disposition

Sun halflings get a +1 to the skill level of either C-Persuasion or C-Performance.

Human

Primary Trait: Astounding Resilience

Humans get a +1 to their level in any one of the four saving throws.

Secondary Trait: Adaptable

Humans get a +1 to any skill level of their choice.

Tertiary Trait: Multicultural

Humans get a +1 to their K-World skill level. In addition, when determining points for language, they get two additional points.

Orc

Orcs are the creation of the celestial Tithys, god of courage and warfare, as well as ground and earth. Orcs are larger and bulkier humanoids, with green, grey, or brown skin. Their eyes tend to be warmer-toned, and they range in height from 6 to 8 feet tall, though grey orcs tend to be a bit shorter and brown orcs tend to be a bit taller. There are three subspecies, green orcs, grey orcs, and brown orcs. Orcs are size Large.

Primary Trait: Powerful

When dealing damage with a melee weapon using your Might, an orc may reroll any one damage dice and must take the new roll. You may use this feature once per turn.

Secondary Trait: Hardy

Green orcs get 1 additional Health per level.

Secondary Trait: Tough

Brown orcs get a +1 natural bonus to their Physical Defence.

Secondary Trait: Resolute

Grey orcs get a +1 natural bonus to their Magical Defence.

Tertiary Trait: Threatening

Orcs get a +2 circumstantial bonus to C-Intimidation checks made towards creatures that are smaller than them.
